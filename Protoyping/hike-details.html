Getting There

Getting there is a little complicated, but you should have no problems. Take a night train or airplane from Stockholm to Kiruna. In Kiruna take a buss to Nikkaloukta. There you can take a helicopter ride (with Kallax Flyg) to your first camp. A helicopter ride to Savujavri costs around 100 euro per person, which is not cheap but well worth it.

Additional Information

The distance between the four first camping places have been kept short (3-5 hours) so there is plenty of time for fishing, exploring the area without a heavy backpack and so on.

The last two days is part of Kungsleden, a famous hike in Sweden, so you will encounter people on that part. On the last day you can take a boat along the lake to gain about a two hour walk. At the end of the boat trip there is a small place that sells reindeer burgers.

There is plenty of pure water around, so no need to bring a purification system if you don't want it. We drank for 7 days from different streams (and a few large lakes) without any problems. Also the locals have been drinking this water for centuries.

Important to note is that there is NO cellphone reception at all for the entire trip, so bring a satelite phone or emergency beacon.
